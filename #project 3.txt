                  <h1>Whats is Cororna?</h1>
              <img class="animated infinite heartBeat delay-2s" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6CO2Xer1Lro/XpykmO36P7I/AAAAAAAABAM/8O-ySFZp2jAIdBC2wWHN05qQd-xYYWprgCNcBGAsYHQ/s1600/corona-logo.png" width="100px" height="90px">
                <p style="color: black;">
                
                    <b>"Corona_viruses"</b> are a group of related viruses that cause diseases in mammals and birds.<br>
                     In humans, coronaviruses cause respiratory tract infections that can range from mild to lethal.<br>
                     Mild illnesses include some cases of the common cold (which has other possible causes, 
                    predominantly rhinoviruses),<br>while more lethal varieties can cause SARS, MERS, and <b>"COVID-19"</b>.<br>
                    There are yet to be vaccines or antiviral drugs toprevent or treat human coronavirus infections.<br>
                
                  <p class="text-center" style="font-size: 20px;"><b>History</b></p>

                <p style="color: black;">

                  <b>"Corona_viruses"</b> were first discovered in the 1930s when an acute respiratory infection of 
                    domesticated chickens was shown to be caused by infectious bronchitis virus (IBV). 
                    In the 1940s, two more animal coronaviruses, mouse hepatitis virus (MHV)<br>
                     and transmissible gastroenteritis virus (TGEV), were isolated.

                  </p>
                  
              </div>
      <hr>

              <div class="precautions text-center" id="Symptomps">
                <h1>Covid-19 In Humans</h1>
                 <img class="animated flipInX delay-4s" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-sa0YLLkEVAk/XpykpgptfRI/AAAAAAAABAc/8zIdB3IZ1cwX1holQRYwoNZJ-yn9Pbd_wCNcBGAsYHQ/s1600/infected-man.png" width="100px" height="90px">

                  <p class="text-center" style="font-size: 20px;"><b style="color:white;">Discovered</b></p>
              <p style="color: black;">      
                  Human <b>"Corona_viruses"</b> were discovered in the <b>1960s</b>.
                 <br>The earliest ones studied were from human patients with the common cold,
                     which were later named human "Corona_viruses" 229E and human coronavirus OC43.
                  <br>They were first imaged by <b>"Scottish virologist"</b> June Almeida at St. Thomas Hospital in London.
                  <br>Other human coronaviruses have since been identified,including<br><b>"SARS-CoV in 2003, 
                  HCoV NL63 in 2004, HKU1 in 2005, MERS-CoV in 2012, and SARS-CoV-2 in 2019.</b> 
                <br>Most of these have involved serious respiratory tract infections.
                  </p>
             <p class="text-center" style="font-size: 20px;"><b style="color:white;">Came In Figure</b></p>
              <p style="color: black;">      

                  In <b>December 2019</b>, a pneumonia outbreak was reported in <b>Wuhan, China.On 31 December 2019,</b> the outbreak was traced to a novel strain of coronavirus, which was given the interim name <b>2019-nCoV</b> by the <b>World Health Organization(WHO),</b>later renamed <b>SARS-CoV-2</b> by the International Committee on Taxonomy of Viruses.<br>Some researchers have suggested the Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market may not be the original source of viral transmission to humans.<br>
                  As of <b>16 April 2020,</b> there have been at least <b>137,078</b> confirmed deaths and more than <b>2,064,815</b> confirmed cases in the <b>coronavirus pneumonia pandemic.</b> The Wuhan strain has been identified as a new strain of Betacoronavirus from group 2B with approximately 70% genetic similarity to the <b>SARS-CoV.</b><br>
             The virus has a 96% similarity to a bat coronavirus, so it is widely suspected to originate from bats as well. The pandemic has resulted in travel restrictions and nationwide lockdowns in several countries.

                  </p>

<!--    ----------------------structure of covid-19--------------------->

              
              <div class="precautions text-center" id="Symptomps">
                <h1>Covid-19 Structure</h1>
                 <img class="animated heartBeat delay-4s" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-NJOi6KOKLP4/XpykuayY7SI/AAAAAAAABA8/PWbnSKGAYGY3lDkvM4BHydYyG03Bbg--wCNcBGAsYHQ/s1600/structure.png" width="200px" height="80px">

                  <p class="text-center" style="font-size: 20px;"><b style="color:white;">Structure</b></p>
              <p style="color: black;">      
                 
                  <b>Coronaviruses</b> are large pleomorphic <b>spherical</b> particles with bulbous surface projections.<br>
                    The average <b>diameter</b> of the virus particles is around <b>120 nm (.12 μm).</b> 
                  The diameter of the envelope is <b>~80 nm (.08 μm)</b> and the spikes are <b>~20 nm (.02 μm)</b> long.<br> 
                    The envelope of the virus in electron micrographs appears as a distinct pair of electron dense shells.<br>
                    The viral envelope consists of a lipid bilayer where the membrane, envelope and spike  structural proteins are anchored. <br>
                    A subset of coronaviruses (specifically the members of <b>betacoronavirus subgroup A</b>) <br> 
                    Inside the envelope, there is the <b>nucleocapsid, </b> which is formed from multiple copies of the nucleocapsid (N) protein,
                     Which are bound to the positive-sense single-stranded RNA genome in a continuous beads-on-a-string type conformation. <br>
                    The lipid bilayer envelope, membrane proteins, and nucleocapsid protect the virus when it is outside the host cell.
      
                  </p>
             <p class="text-center" style="font-size: 20px;"><b style="color:white;">Life cycle of Coronavirus Entry</b></p>
              <p style="color: black;">      

                  Infection begins when the viral spike (S) glycoprotein attaches to its complementary host cell receptor.
                    After attachment, a protease of the host cell cleaves and activates the receptor-attached spike protein. 
                    Depending on the host cell protease available, cleavage and activation allows the virus to enter the host cell by endocytosis or direct fusion of the viral envelop with the host membrane.<br>
                    On entry into the host cell, the virus particle is uncoated, and its genome enters the cell cytoplasm.
                    The coronavirus <b>RNA genome has a 5′ methylated cap and a 3′ polyadenylated tail</b>, which allows the RNA to attach to the host cell's ribosome for translation.<br>
                    The host ribosome translates the initial overlapping open reading frame of the virus genome and forms a long polyprotein. The polyprotein has its own proteases which cleave the polyprotein into multiple nonstructural proteins.
                  
                  </p>

                <b style="color:#e0d863; font-size:17px;">No.2-</b> Some patients may have aches and <b>pains, nasal congestion, runny nose, sore throat or diarrhea.</b> These symptoms                            are usually mild and begin gradually.<br>
